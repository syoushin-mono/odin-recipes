Tamagoyaki

(The steps for this recipe are sourced from this Allrecipes article.)

Description

Tamagoyaki is slightly sweet, and seasoned with soy sauce, mirin, and dashi. It's a favourite in bento boxes and makes an easy appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 tablespoons prepared dashi stock
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 1/2 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Whisk eggs, dashi stock, sugar, mirin, and soy sauce together in a bowl.
  2. Heat 1/3 oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add about 1/3 egg mixture; quickly swirl the pan to evenly cover the bottom. Start rolling up omelette from one side to the other as soon as it is set.
  3. Keep roll to one side; add ⅓ more oil to the skillet and ⅓ more egg. Swirl the pan, ensuring entire bottom is covered, including around and underneath first roll; cook until set. Roll up omelette starting from side with first roll, and including first roll, so you end up with one roll.
  4. Repeat process with remaining 1/3 each oil and egg and so you end up with one roll; transfer to a bamboo rolling mat. Roll up omelette tightly; cool about 3 minutes.
  5. Unwrap omelette; slice into 6 pieces. Serve warm or cold.
